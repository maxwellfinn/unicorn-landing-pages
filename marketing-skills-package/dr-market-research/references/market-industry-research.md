## Market Sizing & Trends

### Market Size Calculation
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: Total market demand
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: Segment you can serve
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: What you can realistically capture

### Calculation Methods
- **Top-down**: Start with total market, apply filters
- **Bottom-up**: Start with unit economics, multiply up
- **Value theory**: Estimate based on value creation

### Data Sources
- Industry reports (IBISWorld, Statista, Grand View Research)
- Government data (Census Bureau, Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- Trade associations
- Market research firms (Gartner, Forrester, Nielsen)
- Financial analyst reports
- Company earnings calls and investor presentations

### Trend Analysis
Research current and emerging trends:
- Growth rate of the market
- Technology disruptions
- Consumer behavior shifts
- Regulatory changes
- Economic factors
- Social and cultural trends
- Environmental considerations

### Key Questions
- Is the market growing, stable, or declining?
- What's the CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)?
- What macroeconomic factors affect this market?
- What new entrants are coming?
- What incumbents are losing ground?
- What innovations are changing the game?

## Industry Analysis

### Porter's Five Forces
Analyze competitive intensity:
1. **Threat of new entrants**: How easy is it to enter this market?
2. **Bargaining power of suppliers**: How much leverage do suppliers have?
3. **Bargaining power of buyers**: How price-sensitive are customers?
4. **Threat of substitutes**: What alternatives exist?
5. **Competitive rivalry**: How intense is the competition?

### Value Chain Analysis
Map the industry value chain:
- Raw materials/inputs
- Production/creation
- Distribution
- Marketing and sales
- Customer service
- Support services

Identify where value is created and captured.

### Industry Life Cycle
Determine the stage:
- **Introduction**: New category, educating market
- **Growth**: Rapid expansion, many new entrants
- **Maturity**: Stable growth, intense competition
- **Decline**: Shrinking market, consolidation

Different stages require different marketing approaches.

### Key Industry Metrics
Research typical benchmarks:
- Average customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Average customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Typical LTV:CAC ratio
- Average conversion rates
- Average order value (AOV)
- Churn rate
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Industry-specific KPIs

## Channel Research

### Marketing Channel Audit
Research which channels are most effective:
- **Paid Search**: Google Ads, Bing Ads
- **Paid Social**: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter, Pinterest
- **Display Advertising**: GDN, programmatic
- **Video Advertising**: YouTube, TikTok, Connected TV
- **Native Advertising**: Taboola, Outbrain
- **Email Marketing**: Cold email, newsletter ads
- **Affiliate Marketing**: Commission-based partners
- **Influencer Marketing**: Partnerships with content creators
- **Content Marketing**: SEO, blog, YouTube organic
- **Traditional Media**: TV, radio, print, direct mail

### Channel Economics
For each viable channel, research:
- Typical cost per click (CPC)
- Typical cost per thousand impressions (CPM)
- Average conversion rate
- Estimated cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Typical return on ad spend (ROAS)
- Saturation level (how crowded is it?)
- Minimum viable budget
- Time to profitability

### Channel-Specific Research Methods
- Browse ad libraries for social platforms
- Use Google Ads Keyword Planner for search volume
- Analyze competitor presence per channel
- Review case studies and industry benchmarks
- Test with small budgets to validate assumptions
- Interview agencies specializing in that channel

### Channel Prioritization Matrix
Score each channel on:
- **Cost**: How expensive is acquisition?
- **Scale**: How much volume is available?
- **Speed**: How fast can you scale?
- **Quality**: How qualified are the leads?
- **Competition**: How saturated is the channel?
- **Fit**: How well does it match your product/market?

## Platform-Specific Research

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- Ad formats that work best for your niche
- Typical CPM and CPC in your industry
- Audience size for your targeting
- Best practices for creative
- iOS 14.5 impact and workarounds
- Audience network performance
- Messenger/WhatsApp opportunities

### Google Ads
- Search volume for money keywords
- Keyword difficulty and competition
- Top of page bid estimates
- Quality score benchmarks
- Shopping ads performance (if e-commerce)
- Display network vs. search network
- YouTube ads creative best practices

### TikTok
- Trending content formats
- Hashtag research
- Influencer landscape
- UGC creative requirements
- TikTok Shop opportunities (if applicable)
- Spark Ads vs. traditional ads

### LinkedIn
- B2B audience availability
- CPC/CPM benchmarks (typically higher)
- Lead gen form performance
- Content marketing opportunities
- Sponsored InMail effectiveness

### YouTube
- Pre-roll vs. mid-roll performance
- Skippable vs. non-skippable
- Bumper ad effectiveness
- YouTube Shorts opportunities
- Influencer partnership landscape

### Platform Research Questions
For each relevant platform:
- What content performs best organically?
- What ad formats are available?
- What targeting options exist?
- What are the creative requirements?
- What's the minimum viable budget?
- What measurement tools are available?
- What's the typical learning phase duration?
- What audiences can you build?

## Seasonal & Timing Analysis

### Seasonality Research
- Monthly demand fluctuations
- Holiday impact (Black Friday, Christmas, etc.)
- Industry-specific seasons (tax season, wedding season, etc.)
- School calendar impact (back-to-school, summer break)
- Weather-related patterns
- Fiscal year patterns (B2B)

### Day-of-Week Patterns
Research when your audience is most active:
- Best days to run ads
- Best days to send emails
- Best days for content publishing
- Worst days to avoid

### Time-of-Day Patterns
- Peak engagement hours
- Time zones to consider
- Optimal ad scheduling windows
- Email send time optimization

### Event-Based Opportunities
- Industry conferences and trade shows
- Sporting events (Super Bowl, World Cup, etc.)
- Awards shows and entertainment events
- Product launches and news cycles
- Economic events (earnings season, Fed meetings)

### Research Methods
- Google Trends analysis
- Historical sales data analysis
- Social media listening for trending topics
- Industry calendar research
- Competitor promotional calendar observation

## Regulatory & Compliance

### Advertising Regulations
Research applicable rules for your industry and location:
- FTC guidelines (US)
- ASA regulations (UK)
- GDPR requirements (EU)
- CAN-SPAM Act (email marketing)
- TCPA (text message marketing)
- Platform-specific advertising policies
- Industry-specific regulations (health, finance, etc.)

### Claim Substantiation
For any claims in advertising:
- What proof is required?
- What disclaimers are needed?
- What language is prohibited?
- What comparative claims are allowed?

### Privacy and Data
- Cookie consent requirements
- Privacy policy requirements
- Data collection and usage rules
- Tracking pixel regulations
- Cross-border data transfer rules

### Financial and Health Claims
Special rules for:
- Income claims and testimonials
- Weight loss and health claims
- Financial advice and returns
- Medical and therapeutic claims
- Results and guarantee language

### Platform Policies
Review advertising policies for:
- Meta Ads
- Google Ads
- TikTok
- LinkedIn
- YouTube
Each has unique restricted content and format requirements.

### Compliance Research Methods
- Review platform policy pages
- Consult with legal counsel
- Review competitor disclaimers
- Study FTC warning letters and cases
- Join industry compliance groups
- Subscribe to regulatory newsletters
