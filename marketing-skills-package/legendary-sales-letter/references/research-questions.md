# Deep Research Questions for Avatar Development

## Overview

These 88 questions guide comprehensive research before writing any sales letter or advertorial. Answer as many as possible through web research, customer review mining, and competitive analysis.

---

## SECTION 1: BUSINESS FOUNDATION (Questions 1-10)

### Product/Service Understanding

1. What is the core product/service offering and primary value proposition?
2. What industry/market does this business operate in?
3. What is the pricing model and tier (budget, mid-market, premium, luxury)?
4. What makes this product/service unique vs. competitors?
5. What problem does it solve at the most fundamental level?

### Business Context

6. How long has this business been operating?
7. What is the company's origin story?
8. Who founded it and why?
9. What is the company's mission or stated purpose?
10. What proof elements are already available (testimonials, case studies, data)?

---

## SECTION 2: DEMOGRAPHICS & FOUNDATION (Questions 11-20)

### Basic Demographics

11. What is the specific age range of ideal customers?
12. What is the gender breakdown?
13. What is the typical income level?
14. What is the education level?
15. What are the common geographic locations?

### Life Situation

16. What family status is most common (single, married, kids, empty nest)?
17. What life stage are they typically in?
18. What is their career level or business situation?
19. Are they B2B or B2C buyers?
20. What is their decision-making role (individual, household, department head)?

---

## SECTION 3: PAIN POINTS & CHALLENGES (Questions 21-35)

### Primary Pain

21. What are the top 3-5 specific problems this product solves?
22. How do customers describe this problem in their own words?
23. What emotional words do they use (frustrated, scared, embarrassed)?
24. How long have they typically suffered with this problem?
25. How does this problem affect their daily life?

### Failed Solutions

26. What solutions have they tried before?
27. Why specifically didn't those solutions work?
28. How much money have they spent on failed solutions?
29. How much time have they invested in failed approaches?
30. What do they blame for their past failures?

### Consequences

31. What happens if they don't solve this problem?
32. What are the immediate consequences of inaction?
33. What are the long-term consequences?
34. Who else is affected by their problem (family, colleagues, customers)?
35. What opportunities are they missing because of this problem?

---

## SECTION 4: GOALS & MOTIVATIONS (Questions 36-50)

### Desired Outcomes

36. What are their primary goals related to this problem area?
37. What specific results would they consider a success?
38. What metrics do they use to measure success?
39. What does their ideal end state look like?
40. What would their life look like if this problem were completely solved?

### Underlying Motivations

41. What motivates them moreâ€”avoiding pain or gaining pleasure?
42. Are they driven by fear, hope, greed, or status?
43. What would solving this problem allow them to do that they can't do now?
44. Who are they trying to impress or satisfy?
45. What would they be proud to tell others about?

### Timeline

46. How urgent is this problem for them?
47. What is their ideal timeline for results?
48. What events or deadlines create urgency?
49. When do they feel this problem most acutely?
50. What would happen if they could solve it immediately?

---

## SECTION 5: DECISION-MAKING PROCESS (Questions 51-60)

### Research Behavior

51. How do they typically research solutions in this category?
52. What sources do they trust for information?
53. How long is their typical buying cycle?
54. Do they compare options extensively or decide quickly?
55. What platforms do they use for research (Google, social, forums)?

### Influences

56. Who else is involved in their decision-making?
57. Whose opinion do they value on this topic?
58. What would others say to discourage the purchase?
59. What would convince the people around them?
60. Do they prefer to buy alone or consult others?

---

## SECTION 6: OBJECTIONS & RESISTANCE (Questions 61-70)

### Common Objections

61. What is their most common objection or concern?
62. What makes them skeptical of solutions in this category?
63. What past negative experiences create hesitation?
64. What risk do they perceive in trying this solution?
65. What's their biggest fear about making the wrong choice?

### Internal Blockers

66. What limiting beliefs prevent action?
67. What excuses do they use for not acting?
68. What do they tell themselves about why "now isn't the right time"?
69. What internal voice talks them out of solutions?
70. What would they need to believe to take action immediately?

---

## SECTION 7: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE (Questions 71-78)

### Current Solutions

71. What alternatives or competitors are they currently using?
72. What do they like about existing options?
73. What do they dislike about existing options?
74. What's missing from what's currently available?
75. What would make them switch from their current approach?

### Market Position

76. What promises/claims have competitors already made?
77. What mechanisms have been presented before?
78. Where are the gaps in competitor messaging?

---

## SECTION 8: COMMUNICATION & BEHAVIOR (Questions 79-88)

### Media Habits

79. Where do they spend time online (platforms, sites, communities)?
80. What influencers or thought leaders do they follow?
81. What publications or media do they consume?
82. What type of content format engages them most?
83. When are they most receptive to marketing messages?

### Language & Tone

84. What language or terminology resonates with them?
85. What tone do they respond to (professional, casual, urgent, calm)?
86. What buzzwords have they become numb to?
87. What communication style builds trust with them?
88. How do they prefer to be communicated with (email, social, video)?

---

## RESEARCH SOURCE GUIDE

### For Customer Voice (Questions 21-35, 61-70):
- Amazon reviews (yours and competitors)
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot reviews
- Reddit discussions in relevant subreddits
- Facebook group conversations
- Quora questions
- YouTube video comments
- Twitter/X discussions
- Support ticket themes
- Sales call objections

### For Demographics (Questions 11-20):
- Website analytics
- Social media insights
- Customer surveys
- Industry reports
- Competitor targeting signals

### For Competition (Questions 71-78):
- Meta Ad Library
- Google Ads Transparency Center
- Competitor websites and landing pages
- Competitor email sequences
- Review sites (what customers compare)

### For Behavior (Questions 79-88):
- Social media platform analysis
- Google Trends
- Industry publications
- Influencer content in the space
- Forum participation patterns

## OUTPUT: THE AVATAR DOCUMENT

After research, compile into:

**Avatar Name**: [Give them a name]

**Demographics**:
- Age, gender, location, income, education, family status
- Job title / business situation

**Problem Statement** (in their words):
"[Direct quote or synthesis of how they describe the problem]"

**Emotional State**:
- Dominant emotion(s)
- How the problem makes them feel
- What they're afraid of

**Failed Solutions**:
- What they've tried
- Why it didn't work
- What they blame

**Desires** (in their words):
"[How they describe their ideal outcome]"

**Objections**:
1. [Primary objection]
2. [Secondary objection]
3. [Tertiary objection]

**Beliefs Required for Purchase**:
- They must believe [belief 1]
- They must believe [belief 2]
- They must believe [belief 3]

**Language to Use**:
- Words they use for the problem
- Words they use for desired outcome
- Phrases that resonate

**Language to Avoid**:
- Overused buzzwords
- Terms that create skepticism
- Tone mismatches

**Where to Reach Them**:
- Platforms
- Publications
- Communities
- Influencers they follow
